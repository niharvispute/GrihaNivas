Understanding Stamp Duty

Stamp duty is a legal tax paid to the government as a proof for any purchase of asset or land. In Mumbai, these rates are determined by the State Government of Maharashtra and are a crucial part of your property acquisition budget.

The calculation is typically based on the higher value between the transaction price and the Ready Reckoner Rate (the government's estimate of the property's value).

Frequently Asked Questions

What are current rates in Mumbai?

expand_more

As of 2024, the standard stamp duty rate in Mumbai is 6% (inclusive of the 1% Metro Cess). Registration fees are capped at ₹30,000 for properties valued above ₹30 Lakhs.

Are there tax benefits?

expand_more

Yes, under Section 80C of the Income Tax Act, you can claim a deduction of up to ₹1.5 Lakhs on stamp duty and registration charges paid during the year of purchase.

When should I pay the stamp duty?

expand_more

Stamp duty must be paid before or at the time of executing the sale deed. Delay in payment can lead to a penalty ranging from 2% to 200% of the deficit amount.
